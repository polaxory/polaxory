# Universe Contract v0

## promise
One social home; every portal leads to a game worth playing.

## product shape
- **Platform:** identity, friends/parties, cosmetics, housing, inventory, transit, discovery, common UI, permissions, analytics, moderation, telemetry, release pipeline.
- **Games:** distinct first-party sectors with independent loops, genres, pacing, economies, and IP.
- **Creator:** later, constrained apartments, shops, RP blocks, quests, hangouts, and micro-worlds built with expressive primitives and strict budgets.
- **Culture:** events, seasons, social status, discovery, fandom, and optional lore hooks.

## invariants
- one universe, many games.
- clean canon is optional; fun is mandatory.
- shared status and social graph may travel.
- gameplay balance, currencies, power progression, and game saves stay local by default.
- platform IP stays coherent; creator RP may be weird inside its sandbox.
- discovery rewards durable quality and session outcomes, not thumbnails or raw clicks.

## non-goals
- no giant empty map as the product.
- no universal economy or global currency.
- no unrestricted creator access to core platform systems.
- no shared-codebase dependency between sectors.

## v1 proof
Two players can party up in a graybox district, enter a genuinely fun 5–10 minute game, return together, hang out briefly, and voluntarily go back in.
